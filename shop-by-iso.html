ISO Grade Selection Guide

How to Choose Your ISO Grade

ISO grades define the quality and performance standards for our products. Lower numbers indicate higher performance and stricter quality controls.

ISO Grade Comparison

ISO Grade Performance Level Typical Applications Price Range
ISO 4 Ultra High Mission-critical operations, advanced research Premium
ISO 5 High Pharmaceutical, biotechnology High
ISO 6 Enhanced Medical devices, precision assembly Moderate-High
ISO 7 Standard Electronics, general manufacturing Moderate
ISO 8 Basic General storage, light industrial Entry-level

Key Considerations

• Regulatory Requirements: Check if your industry has specific ISO grade requirements

• Budget: Higher ISO grades (lower numbers) typically cost more but offer superior performance

• Future Needs: Consider potential future requirements to avoid upgrading later

• Certification: All our products include detailed Certificates of Compliance
